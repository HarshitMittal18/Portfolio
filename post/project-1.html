Project-1: Amazing Mart Analysis

June 10, 2020

This is my first project under Data Analytics. In this, I have leveraged the power of Tableau to create a nice interactive dashboard to analyse the Profit Margins of customers of Amazing Mart with respect to a particular state. By doing so I want to examine the reach of Amazing Mart in the European Markets and to suggest improvements and future strategies. Further, I have also analysed the Sales Target for Amazing Mart for three categories that is furniture, office supplies and technology. This is shown using a Dual Axis Chart. I have used the concept of Data Blending to achieve this visualization.

Visualization - 1

About the Visualization

  • The Map shows all the states where Amazing Mart does its business.
  • The Size of each bubbles indicate the sales volume of that particluar state aggregated for all the customers that have transactions in that particular state.
  • The Color indicates the Profit Margin for that particular state aggregated for all the customers that have transactions in that state.
  • The Second Plot shows the profit margin of individual customers wherein a blue color indicates a profit margin close to 0.5 and a red color indicates a profit margin close to -0.5.

Analysis

  • As we can see from the visualization, Amazing Mart has a good foothold in the European Markets with Germany, France and United Kingdom dominating the sales. These countries also contribute to a major chunk of the profit.
  • Countries like Netherlands, Italy and Ireland wave some red flags as the profit margin for these countries is on the lower side.
  • Many factors can lead to decrease in their profit margin’s like increase in costs of good sold, intense local comptetion in that particular area or maybe company giving excessive discounts.
  • Thus the company needs to reaccess its strategy in these conuntries to increase the profit margin and build a strong customer base.

Visualization - 2

About the Visualization

  • The dual axis chart compares the actual sales with the sales target for each month in three categories that is furniture, office supplies and technology.
  • The second bar chart shows the difference between the actual sales and the sales target.

Analysis

  • Category Furniture: The sales target set by the furniture department increases uniformly every month. The way they have set their targets might not be the best way because they are constantly underperforming and not meeting their targets. A change of strategy is recommended for yeilding better results in future.

  • Category Technology: The technology department is setting targets once per year because of which they might not be accounting for some seasonality effect due to which we can see a lot of red and orange bars clustered together. Again a change of strategy is recomomended.

  • Category Office Supplies: The office supplies department is setting targets quarterly and this by far has yielded the best results. They review their target every four months and after considering all the scenarios they decide on the sales target for the next quarter.

Conclusion:
The Furniture and Technology departments need to revisit their strategy of setting the sales targets and bring some changes to realize their goals.
